Most borrowers understand the basic concept of an offset account: your savings balance reduces the interest charged on your home loan. But few realise just how powerful this feature becomes at higher income levels and with strategic structuring.

## The Mathematics of Offset

Consider a $1.2 million loan at 6.2% with $200,000 in an offset account. You're only paying interest on $1,000,000 — saving approximately $12,400 in interest in the first year alone. Over 30 years, that $200,000 offset balance saves you more than $280,000 in total interest.

## Why It Beats Rate Chasing

A common mistake is choosing a lender solely for their interest rate while ignoring offset capabilities. A loan at 5.99% without offset functionality may cost you more over time than a loan at 6.25% with a fully functional 100% offset account — particularly if you maintain a healthy cash balance.

## Strategic Offset Structuring

For high-net-worth earners and business owners, the strategy goes further:

- **Salary crediting:** Have your income deposited directly into the offset account. Every dollar reduces interest from the day it lands.
- **Business cash reserves:** If your business maintains a float, holding it in a personal offset account (where legally appropriate) can dramatically reduce your home loan interest.
- **Multiple offsets:** Some lenders allow multiple offset accounts linked to a single loan, enabling you to separate savings goals while maximising the interest reduction.

## When to Prioritise Offset Over Rate

As a general rule, if you consistently hold more than $50,000 in savings, offset functionality should be a non-negotiable feature of your loan. We help clients model both scenarios — lower rate without offset vs. slightly higher rate with full offset — to quantify the actual dollar impact.
