Buying your first home in Australia has never been more complex — but the support available has never been greater. Here's a comprehensive guide to every grant, scheme, and concession available to first home buyers in 2026.

## Federal Schemes

### First Home Guarantee (FHBG)
Purchase with as little as 5% deposit without paying Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI). The government guarantees up to 15% of the property value. Available for singles earning up to $125,000 and couples earning up to $200,000 combined.

### Regional First Home Buyer Guarantee
Similar to the FHBG but specifically for buyers purchasing in regional areas. Requires only a 5% deposit and provides government-backed LMI savings.

### First Home Super Saver Scheme (FHSSS)
Withdraw voluntary super contributions (up to $50,000) to put toward your first home deposit. This can provide significant tax advantages compared to saving outside super.

## State-Based Grants

### New South Wales
- $10,000 First Home Owner Grant for new homes up to $750,000
- Stamp duty exemption on properties up to $800,000
- Stamp duty concession on properties $800,000–$1,000,000

### Victoria
- $10,000 First Home Owner Grant for new homes up to $750,000
- Stamp duty exemption on properties up to $600,000
- Stamp duty concession on properties $600,000–$750,000

### Queensland
- $30,000 First Home Owner Grant for new homes (one of the most generous in Australia)
- Transfer duty concessions on properties up to $700,000

### Other States
Each state and territory has its own grant and concession structure. We maintain current details for every jurisdiction and will guide you based on where you're purchasing.
