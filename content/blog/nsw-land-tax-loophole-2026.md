The 2026 revision to NSW land tax thresholds has introduced a significant, though time-limited, opportunity for property investors holding two or more investment properties in New South Wales.

## What Changed

Effective 1 January 2026, the NSW Government adjusted the land tax threshold from $969,000 to $1,075,000 for general taxpayers. While this sounds straightforward, the interaction between the new threshold and existing trust structures has created a planning window that experienced investors should act on before the end of the financial year.

## The Opportunity

If you hold investment properties through a discretionary (family) trust, the new threshold applies independently to each trust structure. This means investors with properties across multiple trusts may see their combined land tax liability reduce — or disappear entirely — if each trust's total land value sits below the new threshold.

## What You Should Do Now

**1. Review your current land valuations.** The Valuer General's determinations from 1 July 2025 are the figures used for the 2026 tax year. If your total holdings are close to the threshold, restructuring may be worthwhile.

**2. Speak with your accountant and broker.** Refinancing a property from personal ownership into a trust requires a new loan — and not all lenders treat trust borrowing the same way. We work with a select group of lenders who specialise in trust lending and can guide you through the credit implications.

**3. Act before June 30.** The current threshold is under review and may not be adjusted upward again in 2027. The window of opportunity is finite.
